Project Summary

TFI Live Re-Imagined is a redesign of Ireland's national public transport app. The project focuses on real-time bus tracking, seamless ticketing, and accessibility improvements. Using the SCAMPER design thinking method, our team proposed data-driven, user-centric features that enhance reliability, inclusivity, and sustainability across Ireland's transport network.

1. Product Choice

What is the idea and what does it do?

The reimagined TFI Live app integrates real-time GPS bus tracking, one-tap mobile ticketing, personalized notifications, and accessibility-focused UI.

Users can:

  • View accurate live bus locations and ETAs
  • Buy and store tickets via Apple Pay or Google Pay
  • Receive vibration or voice alerts for upcoming stops
  • Track CO₂ savings and earn sustainability rewards

What problem does it solve?

The current TFI Live app struggles with:

  • Static timetables that don't reflect delays
  • Complicated multi-step ticket purchases
  • Limited accessibility for older or visually impaired users
  • A lack of personalization or engagement

Our redesign delivers a frictionless, smart, and inclusive travel experience.

Who are the customers?

  • Primary customers: Urban commuters (students and workers) in Dublin and major Irish cities
  • Secondary customers: Tourists and older passengers seeking easy-to-use, reliable transport information

What value does it provide to customers?

  • Time-saving: Live accuracy reduces waiting and missed connections
  • Ease of use: One-tap ticketing and simple navigation
  • Peace of mind: Predictive alerts and "Share My Journey" safety options
  • Inclusivity: Voice and high-contrast modes for accessibility
  • Sustainability: Tracks CO₂ savings and rewards eco-friendly behavior

Who are your competitors?

Competitor Strengths Weaknesses
Google Maps Accurate real-time tracking No ticketing or local personalization
Citymapper Excellent route planning & UX Limited Irish data – only available in Dublin
Current TFI Live Official coverage Outdated interface, limited features
Unique Selling Proposition (USP):
The only Irish public transport app combining real-time GPS tracking, one-tap ticketing, predictive alerts, accessibility-first design, and rewards-based sustainability tracking.

Who/what would need to be involved to make it a reality?

  • Transport for Ireland (TFI): live bus data, ticketing API access
  • Payment providers: Apple Pay, Google Pay, Revolut
  • App developers & UX designers: accessible, intuitive interfaces
  • Data analytics partners: predictive delay and CO₂ tracking
  • Public transport operators: Dublin Bus, Bus Éireann, Luas
  • Accessibility consultants: ensure WCAG 2.2 compliance

2. The Market

2.1 Market Overview

Public transport plays a major role in everyday life across Ireland, especially in Dublin.

According to RTÉ News (June 2024), 58.3% of people entering Dublin city centre during morning rush hours used public transport, while only 25.3% travelled by car. This highlights a strong existing user base that relies on public transport daily.

The National Transport Authority (NTA, 2024) also reported record highs in 2023, with over 145 million journeys made on Dublin Bus and 308 million passenger journeys nationwide on public transport.

This steady rise in demand shows that Ireland's transport users are open to digital solutions that make their journeys easier, more reliable, and more sustainable.

2.2 Market Segmentation

The audience for the TFI Live Re-Imagined app can be divided into several key user segments based on age, behaviour, and goals.

Segment Characteristics Needs Value Proposition
Daily commuters 18–50 years old, students and professionals who use public transport regularly Real-time updates, reliability, minimal delays Saves time with accurate live tracking and predictive alerts
Tourists Visitors using buses or trains for short stays Easy navigation, route suggestions, language clarity Simple interface and built-in city guides for safer, smoother travel
Elderly users 55+ years old, may have visual or physical accessibility needs Clear layouts, voice and vibration features Accessibility-first design with large text, audio alerts, and simple navigation
Eco-conscious citizens 18–35 years old, sustainability-minded individuals Information on emissions saved, eco rewards Displays CO₂ savings and rewards users for sustainable travel choices

Each segment represents a meaningful part of the Irish transport ecosystem, and the redesigned app could cater to all — while focusing on those who use buses and trains most often.

3. Total Addressable Market (TAM)

The Total Addressable Market (TAM) shows how much yearly revenue the new TFI Live Re-Imagined app could make if all target users in its first market (the Greater Dublin Area) used it regularly. This section uses real data about Irish public transport and simple estimates to explain the app's potential scale.

3.1 Key Facts About the Market

Public transport in Ireland is already used by a large number of people every day:

  • County Dublin's population: 1,458,154 (Census 2022)
    Source: Central Statistics Office (CSO, 2023)
  • Greater Dublin Area population: 2,082,605 (2022)
    Source: CSO regional projections via Wikipedia, 2024
  • Dublin Bus passengers in 2023: over 145 million journeys
    Source: National Transport Authority, 2024
  • Total Irish public transport journeys in 2023: more than 308 million, the highest ever
    Source: PML Group / TFI Data, 2024
  • 58.3% of people entering Dublin city centre during morning rush hours used public transport
    Source: RTÉ News, June 2024

These facts show that public transport is a big part of daily life in Dublin, and that there is already a large audience for a better, smarter app.

3.2 Estimating the Beachhead Market

The Beachhead Market is the first group of people who would most likely use the new app — in this case, daily commuters in Dublin who rely on buses or trains and use smartphones.

There isn't exact data on how many people use transport apps in Dublin, but we can make a reasonable estimate using the figures above:

  • About 500,000–700,000 people commute in Dublin every day
  • Around 60–70% of them use public transport
  • Most commuters (about 70–80%) already use apps like Google Maps or TFI Live

That gives a realistic range of about 300,000–400,000 active users, with the possibility of growing to 500,000–700,000 users as the new app adds features like real-time GPS and easy payments.

For this TAM estimate, we use 500,000 users as the main figure.

3.3 Revenue Model Assumptions

The new TFI Live app could stay free to use but earn small amounts from optional and fair monetization, such as:

1. In-App Advertising and Partnerships

  • Local business ads (cafés, shops, or events)
  • "Eco Rewards" sponsors who support sustainable travel
  • Potential revenue: €0.50–€1.00 per user per month

2. Premium Subscription Option

  • Ad-free mode, delay alerts, and CO₂ tracking history
  • Monthly cost: €2.00 per user
  • About 15–20% expected uptake

3. Data Partnerships

  • Aggregated, anonymised data
  • Selling travel pattern insights to councils or research projects
  • Potential: €200,000–€500,000 per year

From these, a realistic average revenue per user (ARPU) would be €2.50 per month.

3.4 TAM Calculation

Metric Estimate
Beachhead Market Size (active Dublin users) 500,000
Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) €2.50 per month
Total Annual Revenue Potential (TAM) 500,000 × €2.50 × 12 = €15 million/year

If the app grows to the higher estimate of 700,000 users, the TAM would increase to €21 million/year.

Once the app succeeds in Dublin, it can expand to other major Irish cities: Cork, Galway, Limerick, and Waterford. Together, these areas have about 1.5 million residents and high levels of bus and train use.

3.6 Strategic Insight

Even small adoption levels could make a real difference.

If only 10% of Dublin commuters (about 50,000 users) paid for premium features, the app could already make about €1.5 million per year.

A fully scaled version across Ireland could reach €40–60 million per year, while helping more people use public transport and supporting national sustainability goals.

5. Quantified Value Proposition (QVP)

The redesigned TFI Live app delivers tangible benefits to Ireland's public transport users by addressing their most pressing pain points with data-driven solutions.

Customer Perspectives

Students

The Challenge: Student commuters face unpredictable bus schedules, long waiting times, and confusing ticketing processes that make commuting stressful and inefficient. Time lost waiting for delayed buses cuts into study time and makes getting to lectures on time unnecessarily difficult.

Working Class Commuters

The Challenge: Working class commuters in Irish cities rely heavily on buses every day to get to and from work. They face unpredictable delays, missed connections, and lose significant time waiting. This can cause much larger work-life balance issues and degrade their general quality of life. Many existing transport apps cannot accommodate the busy lives of these people, and they need a more suitable alternative than static timetables.

What's In It For Them

TFI Live Re-Imagined delivers a faster, simpler, and more inclusive public transport experience:

Save Time & Money

Real-time GPS tracking and live updates show current bus locations and potential delays, eliminating wasted waiting time at stops.

Reduce Stress

Instant delay alerts, pre-emptive alternative routing suggestions, and predictive notifications based on user history help commuters plan ahead.

Improve Accessibility

Voice and vibration settings ensure the app works for all users, regardless of visual or physical accessibility needs.

Streamlined Payments

One-tap ticket purchases via Apple Pay or Google Pay eliminate the hassle of managing physical tickets or multiple payment methods.

Why Buy From Us

Unlike other transportation apps, TFI Live Re-Imagined is designed specifically for Ireland by Irish commuters who understand the unique challenges of the Irish public transport system.

Irish-First Features

  • Real-time Irish bus data: Integrated directly with Transport for Ireland's network for accurate, up-to-the-minute information
  • Weather updates: Irish weather patterns integrated into journey planning and delay predictions
  • Quick payment options: One-tap tickets via Apple Pay and Google Pay
  • Eco tracking and rewards: Track CO₂ savings and earn rewards for sustainable travel choices
  • Clean, ad-free experience: Premium users enjoy an uncluttered interface focused purely on their journey

These features combine convenience and sustainability in a way currently available apps don't, creating a genuinely Irish solution to Irish transport challenges.
